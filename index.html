About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Finance at the University of Iowa's Tippie College of Business, on the academic job market in 2026–2027. My research sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence and empirical finance: I develop deep-learning and large-language-model methods to detect hidden corporate fraud, predict stock returns, and measure managerial behavior from text at scale.

Research

Job Market Paper

Future Proves Past: Detecting Hidden Fraud under Imperfect Enforcement Labels

A deep convolutional neural network that learns from future accounting outcomes to uncover economically meaningful accounting fraud beyond the SEC's enforcement record.

with C. Wei Li, Yuxuan Li, and Anand M. Vijh

Working Paper

Listening to History Rhymes: Pattern Recurrence and Return Predictability in Stock Markets

Using the Matrix Profile to detect recurrent price patterns and machine-learning models to turn them into out-of-sample return forecasts.

with C. Wei Li

Working Paper

CEO Individualism and the Distribution of Performance Risk

Measuring CEO individualism from earnings-call speech with transformer embeddings and a fine-tuned (QLoRA) large language model, and linking it to how firms allocate downside risk to workers.

solo-authored

Working Paper

When “Milliseconds” Matter: The Real Effects of High-Frequency Trading

How high-frequency trading reshapes corporate investment and external financing, identified from a high-speed market-data feed and exchange co-location rollouts.

with Wesley Deng, Bo Hu, and Amy Kwan

Work in progress on frequency-domain asset pricing and LLM-based analysis of executive contracts and compensation governance.
